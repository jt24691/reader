The History of the Jeep





The Jeep was first developed as a general purpose vehicle for the U.S. Army. It was shortened to G.P. on the paperwork and was referred to as a G. P. in Army slang.
When General Motors began to sell them to the public, they were so used to being called G.P.s that the company called them Jeeps instead.
The first Jeep was called the Willy it served from WW2 to the Korean war.
Then other models like the Commanders, Liberty, Renegade, Patriot, and the newer truck.
